Fluency Disorders

Fluency disorders exist when there is an interruption in the rhythm or rate of speech that is characterized by hesitations, repetitions or prolongations of sounds, syllables, words or phrases that interferes with communication.

Secondary or "struggle" behaviors may be present such as eye blink, head turn, tension in the neck or face etc.

The goal of intervention is to help return control of fluent speech production back to the speaker.

Childhood Speech and Language Disorders

  • Articulation Disorder: Characterized by difficulty forming and producing developmentally appropriate speech sounds.
  • Phonological Disorder: Displaying difficulty making developmentally appropriate speech sounds and a difficulty with ordering these sounds into words. Apraxia and dysarthria disorders may be present.
  • Receptive Language Disorder: Characterized by a difficulty in understanding language.
  • Expressive Language Disorder: May present itself with difficulty in one or more of the areas of syntax (grammar), semantics (vocabulary), and pragmatics (social language).
